Acoustofluidic technologies for precision biomedicine.

I develop sound-powered microdevices that manipulate biological samples from extracellular vesicles and cells to tissue-level delivery interfaces, with applications in cytopathological screening, liquid biopsy, single-cell manipulation and non-invasive transdermal drug delivery.

Research identity

A profile built around engineering innovation, biomedical validation and translation toward practical medical devices.

Cytometry

Multi-view acoustic rotation cytometry

Developing acoustic cell rotation and deformation approaches for pre-cytopathological screening and urological cancer detection.

Liquid biopsy

Extracellular vesicle enrichment

Building ultrafast acoustic enrichment platforms for nanoparticles and extracellular vesicles in cancer and infectious disease diagnostics.

Drug delivery

Acoustic-droplet transdermal delivery

Creating acoustic droplet-based permeation strategies for rapid, non-invasive and controllable delivery across tissue barriers.

Research interests

My work combines acoustics, microfluidics, bioengineering and translational device development to address biomedical problems where sample handling, diagnostic sensitivity or delivery efficiency are limiting factors.

Acoustofluidic devices Extracellular vesicles Liquid biopsy Cell manipulation Single-cell printing Transdermal delivery Medical device translation

Core expertise

Acoustofluidic engineering and system integration

Acoustic/microfluidic platform design, microfabrication, transducer integration, CAD/3D prototyping, COMSOL simulation, acoustic-field analysis and device characterisation.

Biomedical experimental validation

Mammalian cell culture, H&E staining, fluorescence microscopy, flow cytometry, cryosectioning, extracellular vesicle handling and image-based quantitative analysis.

Translation and leadership

Grant writing, interdisciplinary collaboration, industry-facing technology development, early-stage commercialisation and student mentoring.
